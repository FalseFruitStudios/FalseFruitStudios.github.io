What Is 3D Printing?

So what is 3D printing ?

3D printing is the commonname used when referring to the current generation of additive manufacturing equpiment, espcially the home tier machines that have rapidly gained traction within hobbyist communities.

Common 3D printers function by melting plastic "filament" commonly made of ABS or PLA to a high temprature, and then extruding the molten plastic to build a 3D object layer by layer from an original digital model.

The additive nature of 3D printing means that far less materials are neccessary to create a object when compared with traditional subtractive manufacturing such as CNC carving.

3D printing enables you to produce complex functional shapes using less material than traditional manufacturing methods.
